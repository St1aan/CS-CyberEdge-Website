Your Social Accounts Are More Exposed Than You Think

Most social media accounts were set up years ago with default settings, weak recovery options, and a growing list of forgotten third-party apps that still hold full access to your data. Every one of those is a potential entry point for an attacker.

SocialDefender takes a systematic approach to auditing each account, identifying the gaps, and giving you a clear, prioritised plan to close them — written for people, not security professionals.

Average user has 50+ third-party apps with live access to their social accounts

Account takeover is the fastest-growing form of identity theft, largely via social platforms

Recovery options are frequently the weakest link — old phone numbers, inactive email addresses

Default privacy settings expose far more personal data than most users realise

What We Assess

Every SocialDefender assessment covers six security domains across all your active social accounts.

Privacy & Visibility Settings

A line-by-line review of who can see your posts, profile information, tagged content, and contact details — with recommended settings for your risk profile.

Connected Apps & Third-Party Access

Enumerate every application with OAuth access to your accounts. Identify dormant, high-risk, or unknown apps and provide a guided revocation process.

Login History & Active Sessions

Review active sessions, recent login locations, and device history to identify any suspicious or unrecognised access to your accounts.

Account Recovery Hardening

Verify that recovery email addresses, backup phone numbers, trusted devices, and backup codes are current, valid, and under your control.

Authentication & MFA Review

Assess the strength of multi-factor authentication in use across each platform, and recommend upgrades from SMS-based to app-based or hardware key MFA.

Impersonation Exposure Check

Search for lookalike accounts and profiles impersonating you or your brand across major platforms, and provide evidence for takedown requests.

Legal & Data Handling

SocialDefender is a confidential, point-in-time assessment. We take data protection and client trust extremely seriously.

  • • Minimum necessary access: We only request the access and data required to perform the six-domain review. We prefer read-only exports, delegated access, or guided walkthroughs over broad credentials.
  • • No passwords stored: We never ask for or store passwords. All access is temporary and revoked after the engagement.
  • • Confidentiality: All client information is handled under strict confidentiality. A mutual NDA is available before work begins.
  • • Data retention: Evidence is stored securely for the duration of the engagement and deleted (or returned) within 30 days of final report delivery unless you request longer retention in writing.
  • • Your control: You remain in full control of your accounts at all times. We do not make changes without your explicit approval.

This is a professional service engagement, not a guarantee against future incidents. The assessment reflects the state of your accounts at the time of review.
